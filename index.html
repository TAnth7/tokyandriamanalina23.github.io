I am Toky, a Malagasy PhD student at the University of Potsdam (Germany). My work is done under the supervision of Prof. Dr. Myfanwy Evans and Dr. Sonia Mahmoudi, in the team of Applied Geometry and Topology. I completed my Bachelor's and Magisterium at the university of Strasbourg (France), and my Master's at the University of Rennes 1 (France).

My PhD project is about the topological and geometrical study of triply periodic knotted structures (see and play with the 3D model). I use Houdini to visualise such structures, and use mathematical tools that stem from Knot theory to understand the entanglement.

I am interested in using mathematics to understand biological and molecular structures, or any other object presenting physical properties driven by its topological or geometrical features. Here I give a non-exhaustive list of my interests: pure/applied topology and geometry, knot theory, weavings, knittings, algebra, crystallography, biology, physics, chemistry, 3D animation and visualisation.
